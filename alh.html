For the Curator and Exhibition Manager position
Art League Houston

What has kept me in and around contemporary art for the past fifteen years is, at its core, an interest in what happens between a work and the people who encounter it — between an artist and an audience, between an exhibition and the community that receives it. Art League Houston is organized around that question. It's embedded in the mission, and it's what draws me to this role.

Over that time I've worked as Gallery Director at Laura Rathe Fine Art, as founder and director of Gallery Nua, and currently as a gallery associate at Reeves Art Gallery. Across all of it, I've developed the full range of experience this position requires: exhibition planning and production, hands-on installation, budget and schedule management, artist contracts and logistics, catalogue production, and public programming. What I've found is that the operational work and the relational work can't really be separated — the quality of an exhibition often comes down to how well the relationships around it have been tended.

The Curator and Exhibition Manager role spans a broad range of responsibilities, from installation and art handling to donor engagement, from artist talks to gala logistics. The range of people it involves — artists, benefactors, community members, board members, institutional partners — is one of the things I find most compelling about it. I've worked across that spectrum, and I tend to be most effective when a role requires holding multiple registers at once.

I'd welcome the opportunity to talk further about the role and what working with ALH might look like.

Sincerely,
Aidan J. Sinclair
